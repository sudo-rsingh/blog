Introduction

If I said that I only follow that quote and invest based on that, that would be very foolish and outright a major lie that can cause major financial damage to the follower. I will take some time and explain the quote by Buffet, he essentially means there that when people are selling and the price of the stock is crashing we should buy at that time and vice versa. But is it a bit of sound advice to just buy anything that the majority people are wary of, that would be dumb on multiple levels. I will illustrate an example with this same advice working and an example when this advice is not working in upcoming sections.
A very thorough analysis is always required before making any purchase or sale of stocks which should not be neglected under any circumstances. New and active investors have a tendency to make fast purchases on identifying a good opportunity, while it is suggested that we launch towards a good chance it is not uncommon to see that many good purchases fail when we do things hastily, this can be very well explained by the current market conditions where we can see that the NIFTY50 is going at the top but the overall market is down and can even be said that it is in miserable condition. Some people might think of this as an opportunity while others might think of it as a major obstacle. My stance over this is much more complicated than these two options, I see it as an opportunity, not an opportunity to make money but an opportunity to learn how to make money, this situation is a common trend of the market which happens over a 15-20 years time frame and there is so much to learn from it because there are plenty of pitfalls that are dug by the market and any untrained person who wants to make quick money will fall into it.

Escaping the high charges of the brokers

This section will matter more to the people who might be involved in day trading, options, or future trading. Regardless I felt that this is an important thing that normal people who just want to do delivery trade should also be aware of. In earlier times when the orders used to be placed by human interventions there used to be human brokers and they charged absurd prices and many times recommended stocks with fraudulent fundamentals. This majorly stopped when discount brokers were introduced, they did not have such a large staff so they could offer the placing of orders with very low charges. Even then I would always say to go to the broker who gives the lowest charges. One thing that you will notice when you try out multiple brokers is that even when they say that they charge 0 brokerage fee there is still a difference between the total fee of different brokers.
Now some of the new traders might be thinking that anyways brokerage fee is a small part of their total investment and it does not matter in the long run
